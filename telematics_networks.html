Telematics and Networks I

Data transmission has evolved from analog to digital signals, overcoming attenuation problems and leveraging advantages in speed and security. To connect equipment to the network, devices such as modems or adapters are used to convert signals. Telematics studies the transmission of information through computer networks, impacting our way of perceiving, learning, and communicating. This course will address the fundamentals of networks, digital transmission, and simulations in Packet Tracer for practical learning. Students will master network protocols, routing, switching, and network security while gaining hands-on experience with Cisco equipment and network simulation tools.

Learning Objectives

  • Recognize the characteristics of the IP protocol and its operation on the Internet, performing calculations for different IP addresses
  • Define the concepts of instrumentation and network systems, systematically explaining the components of an IP system
  • Identify different wired and wireless transmission media and their advantages for network testing and cable assembly
  • Explain the operation of an Ethernet network and its control mechanisms according to network equipment functionality
  • Design and implement computer networks, including configuration of network devices, routers, switches, and ensuring effective communication
  • Implement security protocols such as VPN or encryption to protect communication
  • Diagnose and solve problems in telecommunications and computer systems
  • Evaluate network planning, implementation, and documentation considering efficiency, security, and scalability

Course Outline

    Topic 1: Introduction to Telematics
    • Introduction and general concepts
    • Network diagram interpretation
    • Network topologies: bus, star, ring, and mesh
    • Types of attacks and physical security
    • Electronic communication concepts
    • Physical components of a network

    Topic 2: Data Communication Standardization
    • OSI and TCP/IP reference models
    • Layer 1 to Layer 7 overview
    • IP addressing and address fields
    • Transport layer functions: TCP and UDP
    • Segmentation and flow control
    • Three-way handshake and windowing
    • Well-known and registered ports

    Topic 3: Cisco Equipment Operation
    • ARP tables and addressing
    • CSMA/CD standards and switching
    • Layer 2 and Layer 3 addressing
    • Switch configuration and CLI
    • Telnet and SSH access
    • Routing between network devices
    • LAN vs WLAN differences
    • 802.11 wireless standards

    Topic 4: Routing, Addressing, and Layer 3 Configuration
    • IPv4 configuration and routing
    • Quality of Service (QoS)
    • 802.11 topology components
    • EIGRP and OSPF routing protocols
    • DHCP and FTP network services
    • Static and dynamic routing for LAN

    Topic 5: Telematics Services
    • WAN devices and cabling
    • HDLC, PPP, and Frame Relay protocols
    • RIPv1 and RIPv2
    • Cisco IOS image management
    • VPNs and network tunnels
    • Network security and firewalls
    • VLANs and network segmentation
    • Network troubleshooting
