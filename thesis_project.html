Thesis Project

The Thesis Project is the culminating academic experience for Computer Systems Engineering students, demonstrating mastery of technical skills and research methodology. Students will undertake their thesis projects in high-performance computing, simulation, AI, or graphics, significantly increasing the quality and complexity of their research. This course guides students through the complete research process, from topic selection and proposal development to implementation, experimentation, and thesis defense. Students work closely with faculty advisors to produce original contributions to the field, whether through novel algorithms, system implementations, or comprehensive performance analyses.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and formulate a significant research problem in computer systems
  • Conduct comprehensive literature review and critical analysis
  • Design and execute rigorous experimental methodologies
  • Implement complex systems or algorithms demonstrating technical depth
  • Analyze results using appropriate statistical and evaluation techniques
  • Write a formal thesis document following academic standards
  • Present and defend research findings to an academic committee
  • Contribute original knowledge to the field of computer systems engineering

Course Outline

    Research Planning:
    • Topic selection and problem formulation
    • Literature review and state-of-the-art analysis
    • Research questions and hypotheses
    • Thesis proposal development and defense

    Implementation and Experimentation:
    • System design and architecture
    • Implementation and development
    • Experimental setup and methodology
    • Data collection and analysis

    Analysis and Validation:
    • Results analysis and interpretation
    • Performance evaluation and benchmarking
    • Statistical significance testing
    • Comparison with existing approaches

    Documentation and Defense:
    • Thesis writing and documentation
    • Technical diagrams and visualizations
    • Presentation preparation
    • Thesis defense and examination

    Potential Research Areas:
    • High-performance computing and parallel algorithms
    • Computer graphics and visualization
    • Artificial intelligence and machine learning systems
    • Network protocols and distributed systems
    • Database systems and query optimization
    • Cybersecurity and cryptography
    • Cloud computing and virtualization



Students will be able to undertake their thesis projects in high-performance computing, simulation, AI, or graphics, significantly increasing the quality and complexity of their research. GPU infrastructure enables thesis projects that push the boundaries of what's computationally feasible, whether implementing novel parallel algorithms, developing real-time graphics systems, training large-scale AI models, or simulating complex physical systems. Access to GPU computing resources allows students to conduct research that rivals work done at major universities and research labs, preparing them for graduate studies or advanced technical positions in industry.
