Industrial Engineering Projects

The Industrial Engineering Project focuses on optimizing complex processes, systems, and organizations. Students apply data analytics, operations research, and human factors engineering to improve efficiency, quality, and productivity in manufacturing and service industries.

Research Proposals

sensors

IoT Data Collection for Predictive Maintenance

Deploying a network of IoT vibration and temperature sensors to create a robust dataset. This data is used to train Supervised Machine Learning models that predict equipment failures before they occur, minimizing downtime.

precision_manufacturing

RL-Driven Production Scheduling

Utilizing real-time production data collected from IoT-enabled machines to train Reinforcement Learning agents. The system dynamically optimizes job scheduling and resource allocation to maximize throughput in an Industry 4.0 environment.

visibility

Automated Quality Control via Computer Vision

Integrating IoT cameras into the assembly line to collect image data of manufactured parts. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are trained to detect surface defects in real-time, ensuring high quality standards.

energy_savings_leaf

Industrial Energy Optimization using ML

Aggregating energy consumption data from smart meters across the factory floor. Unsupervised learning models analyze this IoT data to identify inefficiencies and recommend operational changes to reduce the carbon footprint.
