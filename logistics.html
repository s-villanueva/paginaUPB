Integral Logistics

Integral Logistics comprehensively addresses all processes that enable the management of the flow of goods, services, or information throughout the entire network of operations within an organization. Proper determination of these processes is key within any company, as they include various activities and decisions. It is vital to know and perform adequate planning, organization, direction, and control of the entire supply system to satisfy internal needs (from supplier management, supply scheduling, inventory control, and material distribution) as well as the physical distribution system focused on outbound logistics to satisfy final consumer needs. Current competition requires companies to have excellent fluidity and synchronization to be notable in the market, optimizing the cash-to-cash cycle while delivering the best service.

Learning Objectives

  • Achieve theoretical learning about the correct functioning of different logistics systems at all levels
  • Promote active learning through real-world experience in solving industrial logistics problems
  • Understand the different links of a logistics system, their roles, and interaction
  • Clearly determine supply strategies in complex supply chains
  • Familiarize with different methods to design robust supply chains
  • Describe and characterize a logistics network with all its channels
  • Design logistics supply and distribution systems
  • Identify indicators and consolidate them in a balanced scorecard for proper logistics planning control

GPU-accelerated computing revolutionizes logistics optimization by enabling real-time analysis of complex supply chain networks with thousands of nodes and millions of possible routes. Students can leverage GPU infrastructure to solve large-scale vehicle routing problems, optimize warehouse layouts using advanced simulation, and perform predictive analytics on massive logistics datasets. With campus GPU resources, learners can implement machine learning models for demand forecasting, analyze real-time IoT sensor data from transportation fleets, and optimize multi-echelon inventory systems that would be computationally prohibitive on traditional hardware. This computational power enables students to tackle logistics challenges at the scale faced by companies like Amazon, DHL, and Maersk, preparing them for careers in modern supply chain management where data-driven decision-making is essential.
