Production and Operations Management

Production Engineering is a key area for improving productivity in any type of company. Establishing a creative advantage through operations requires understanding how operations and supply chain functions contribute to productivity growth. Production engineering deals with the design, operation, and improvement of systems that create and provide a company's primary products and services. Without effective operations management, a modern industrialized society cannot exist. In any organization, the operations function is the engine that creates company profits and supports the country's growth. This course focuses on the fundamentals and decisions of production engineering, strategy, productivity measures, process design and selection, demand forecasting, capacity planning, and plant layout.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the importance of value, design, and execution of operations for greater productivity
  • Establish the operations strategy of a production system
  • Comprehend principles for selecting a production process for goods or services
  • Apply analysis for measuring process flows and identifying bottlenecks
  • Apply qualitative and quantitative methods for demand forecasting
  • Determine fundamentals and principles for production capacity planning
  • Understand fundamental principles for plant location and layout
  • Apply lean production systems, Just in Time, Total Quality Control, and Six Sigma
