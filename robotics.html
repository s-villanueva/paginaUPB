Robotics

Robotics is an essential part of developing competencies in electromechanical systems. This course presents the concepts, techniques, and technological tools available to address topics such as: analysis of different types of robots, kinematics and dynamics of robots, control and navigation of mobile robots. Students will learn to model and simulate robotic systems, understand mechanical limitations of robotic structures, and design, model, and control both mobile and articulated robots. The course combines theoretical foundations with practical implementation using simulation software like CoppeliaSim.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and analyze different types of robots and their applications
  • Understand the mechanical limitations of robotic structures
  • Model and simulate the behavior of a robot's end effector
  • Design, model, and control mobile and articulated robots
  • Apply homogeneous transformations in the robotic context
  • Implement Kalman filtering for mobile robot localization
  • Use simulation software for robot navigation
  • Understand perception in the robotic context using sensors, radars, and cameras

Course Outline

    Unit 1: Simple Open-Chain Robots and Geometric Models
    • Introduction to Robotics and Mechanisms
    • Degrees of freedom of a robot
    • Transformation matrices in 3D
    • Homogeneous transformation matrices
    • Direct Geometric Model (DGM)
    • Inverse Geometric Model (IGM)
    • Open-chain robot modeling

    Unit 2: Control and Motion Generation
    • Kinematic models (Khalil-Kleinfinger)
    • Joint types and characteristics
    • Dynamic models of open-chain robots
    • Mobile robots: theory and applications
    • Localization of mobile robots
    • Control and motion generation
    • Perception: sensors, radars, and cameras
    • Image processing for robotics

    Unit 3: Localization of Mobile Robots
    • Localization concepts and mapping
    • Probability concepts for robotics
    • Markov localization approach
    • Kalman filter for localization
    • SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)
    • Simulation in CoppeliaSim



Integrating GPU-accelerated AI tools into this Robotics course dramatically enhances the learning experience by enabling real-time simulation of complex robotic systems, rapid computation of forward and inverse kinematics, and high-fidelity dynamic modeling. Students can leverage GPU computing to train neural network-based controllers, implement deep reinforcement learning for autonomous navigation, and process sensor data from cameras and LiDAR in real-time. AI-driven computer vision algorithms powered by GPUs enable advanced object detection, scene understanding, and SLAM implementations. With campus GPU infrastructure, learners can experiment with state-of-the-art techniques like transformer-based motion planning, neural radiance fields for 3D reconstruction, and end-to-end learning approaches that are revolutionizing modern robotics in manufacturing, autonomous vehicles, and service robotics.
